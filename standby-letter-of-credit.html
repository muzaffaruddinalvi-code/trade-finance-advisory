Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC)

A Standby Letter of Credit (SBLC) is a financial guarantee issued by a bank to support contractual or payment obligations. It is typically used as a fallback mechanism, where payment is triggered only if the applicant fails to meet agreed terms.

In simple terms: An SBLC provides assurance to the beneficiary that obligations will be fulfilled, or payment will be made under defined conditions.

Common Commercial Uses

  • Performance guarantees in contracts
  • Security for advance payments
  • Support for leasing or infrastructure agreements
  • Trade-related contractual obligations

Key Characteristics

  • Non-primary payment instrument (used as a guarantee)
  • Triggered only upon default or non-performance
  • Issued by a bank subject to credit and compliance review
  • Widely used in international commercial agreements

When to Consider an SBLC

  • Your counterparty requires a financial guarantee
  • You are entering a contract with performance obligations
  • Advance payments require security
  • There is a need to strengthen contractual credibility
