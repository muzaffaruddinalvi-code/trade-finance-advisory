Common Challenges in International Trade

  • Payment risk with overseas buyers or suppliers
  • Lack of trust between new trading partners
  • Requirement for financial guarantees
  • Cash flow constraints impacting transactions

Structured Trade Finance Solutions

  • Letters of Credit (LC) – Secure payment mechanism
  • Standby Letters of Credit (SBLC) – Financial guarantees
  • Bank Guarantees – Contractual security
Solutions are aligned based on transaction structure, counterparty requirements, and banking review.

When to Reach Out

  • Your supplier requests an LC
  • Your contract requires a bank guarantee or SBLC
  • You are entering a new international trade relationship
  • You need structured support for a transaction

How the Process Works

  1. Initial discussion to understand transaction
  2. Preliminary structuring alignment
  3. Introduction to authorized banking team
  4. Bank-led compliance and approval process
